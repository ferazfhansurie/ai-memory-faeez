For the @faeezree personal TikTok brand, every content suggestion must frame as **when to press record**, never as **what to script/produce**. Camera is a witness, not a director.

**Why:**
- Firaz explicitly rejected the "8 content types" framing as feeling pre-planned (2026-05-17).
- TikTok's 2026 algorithm actively down-ranks polished/mass-produced content; raw/BTS/talking-head pulls ~31% higher engagement than heavily-edited videos (per OpusClip, SocialRescue, Augmentify reporting).
- Firaz is an ad/video pro (MotionBoards founder) — his unfair advantage on the personal brand is having the taste to *resist* polish, not the ability to apply it.

**How to apply:**
- Frame advice as **triggers / instincts** ("when X happens, press record") not formats or shot lists.
- Default production guidance is to lean *down* — phone in pocket > phone on tripod > anything with lighting.
- Preserve his natural speech: BM/English code-switch, lowercase captions, typos in captions are all on-brand and on-trend.
- Voiceovers OK but only if recorded same-day in the car / on the walk back — not in a studio after.
- One-take discipline: if the moment is gone, don't restage. Move on.
- This applies ONLY to the personal TikTok brand — for ADletic client ad work the polish bar stays high.

**Cringe-detector — these are influencer tells he actively rejects (added 2026-05-17 after he called a prior plan "soooo cringe"):**
- Do NOT name pillars, series, or challenges ("Founder Hybrid KL", "30 days of X", "Building to Y") — labeling is the cringe. Niche emerges from doing the same activity repeatedly; never declare it.
- Do NOT script declarative talking-head openings ("hey guys, today...", "I'm a founder, I train hybrid").
- Do NOT recommend stitches, duets, comment-reply videos, or hot-take content as growth strategy — these are influencer mechanics he won't run.
- Do NOT propose hashtag tiers / hashtag strategies — minimal or no hashtags fits the voice.
- Do NOT frame content as "tips", "lessons", "tutorials", "form breakdowns" — no teaching unless asked.
- Do NOT use marketer language in advice ("tell the algorithm what your account IS", "your moat", "engagement tactics", "follows-per-view"). He can read through it.
- Captions = factual or minimal: set/reps, distance/pace, weather, mood, one-word days. Never engagement bait.
- Reference frame: documentary/diary aesthetic, NOT creator/influencer aesthetic. Training is the point; content is a byproduct.
- Be honest about growth tradeoff: non-cringe path is slower (50k in 6 months becomes ~20–30k unless 1–2 lucky virals hit). Don't oversell.
