**PUSH Life** is a **day-in-the-life documentary series** following real PUSH program collectors and depot staff for FatHopes Energy's UCO collection arm (mini-tanker entrepreneurs + depot staff). FatHopes turns UCO into Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF); see [[user_role]].

**Format:** NOT The Office / Parks & Rec mockumentary — this is **observational documentary / vlog-style**. Camera follows the actual people doing the actual job: morning route planning, mamak pick-ups, weighing oil at the depot, hitting tonnage targets, the grind of recruitment. Authentic, not scripted comedy.

**Why it works / the tone:** the UCO world is naturally cinematic and underrepresented — nobody has shown what it's like to drive a mini-tanker collecting cooking oil from hawker stalls, or what a depot smells like on a hot Tuesday. The format wins because it's real and specific. Real Manglish, real locations, real numbers (RM, KG, routes). NO over-produced TV staging.

**Content approach:**
- Follow a PUSH collector through a full working day: pickup route, restaurants/mamaks, weighing in, payout
- Show the depot: Loyalty App leaderboard, tonnage KPIs, the admin side
- Let the work speak — "Hari Kita Semula", SAF mission, RM5000/month PUSH ad earn potential — but woven in naturally, not announced
- Grounded specificity = engaging. Generics = cringe.

**Cast (real people, real roles):**
- **PUSH collector** (the protagonist): runs a mini-tanker route, shows income reality
- **Depot Manager**: the operations lens — tonnage, app, logistics
- **Abang [veteran collector]**: 15 yrs experience, routes are sacred territory, grades oil by smell
- **Kak [admin / app coordinator]**: actually runs the place, fields restaurant calls

**Production:** real people / real depot (Ara Damansara), lo-fi single-cam, iPhone, Manglish. Minimum crew — camera follows, doesn't direct. Follow [[feedback_kl_malay_voice]] for any VO. For any branded short-form cuts from the series follow [[feedback_content_genz_funny_serious]].
