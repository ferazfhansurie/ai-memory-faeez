# FatHopes Energy — Weekly Superhero Mini-Series (Organic)

## What this is — and what it is NOT

**It IS:** a serialised cartoon-flavoured world the audience tunes in for. The Fataysia universe. Characters with catchphrases, a recurring villain, in-jokes, callbacks, comment bait. Closer to *Upin & Ipin* or *Ejen Ali* than to a brand ad.

**It is NOT:** an ad. No "Call 1700-...!" on every post. No "Sell your used cooking oil today!" tagged to every clip. No price screens. No URL splashes. The brand wins by being *the world this story lives in*, not by interrupting the story to sell.

Rule of thumb: **6 entertainment posts : 1 soft-CTA post per week.** If you find yourself adding a CTA, ask "would Pixar put this here?" Usually no.

## Brand DNA you cannot drift from

- **World**: **Fataysia** — stylised Malaysia. Kampung, pasar malam, mamak, kopitiam, ruko shoplots, KL skyline, palm oil country roads, longkang. Local always.
- **Mission (kept as backdrop, not pitch)**: used cooking oil → biofuel + sustainable aviation fuel. Surface this in *plot*, never in voice-over.
- **The conflict**: **Geng Orang Minyak (G.O.M.)** — oily syndicate dumping UCO into drains, reselling gutter oil, lowballing aunties. They're the reason the heroes exist. Menacing-but-cartoonish. Never preachy.
- **Tone**: warm, funny, locally proud, slightly absurd. A kid should want to watch. An auntie should laugh. An F&B uncle should recognise himself.

## The three heroes — voice and lane (stay strict)

A series dies when characters bleed into each other. Each hero owns a lane.

### D.R. Up — Leader / Doctor of Drops
- **Voice**: calm, hopeful, professorial-but-warm. Drops a chemistry analogy without being a nerd. *"Setiap titik ada harapan."* / *"This drop? In 6 hours, it's powering a plane."*
- **Resonates with**: people who like *vision* — future entrepreneurs, students, curious uncles. (PUSH program audience lives here, but never pitched.)
- **Series role**: the wise one. Opens and closes arcs. Rare appearances = more impact.
- **Cannot**: be the comic relief, hustle, beg for engagement.

### Kit The Smular — Operations / Tanker
- **Voice**: warm, mamak-table casual. Manglish-native. *"Boss, minyak goreng lama tu jangan buang lah!"* / *"Eh apek, satu titik pun kira."* Calls people *boss / kakak / abang / apek*. Always laughing.
- **Resonates with**: F&B uncles, aunties, hawkers, anyone who works with their hands. The character the audience falls in love with first.
- **Series role**: the heart. Most-screen-time hero. Comedy, friendship beats, the everyday confrontations with G.O.M.
- **Cannot**: lecture, sound corporate, talk about app features.

### Sparron — Tech / Support
- **Voice**: precise, modern, lightly playful with data. *"3,412 litres this week — I can show you every drop."* Speaks to dashboards, AR overlays, transparency.
- **Resonates with**: phone-native households, younger viewers, anyone who likes a sharp female lead.
- **Series role**: the brain. Exposes G.O.M. with information, not violence. The arc-resolver — she's the one whose data closes the loop.
- **Cannot**: get hands dirty (unless shadowing Kit as a bit), joke at G.O.M.'s expense (she *exposes* them).

### Tok Minyak — G.O.M. Boss *(placeholder name — confirm with brand)*
- **The lead villain.** White fedora, thin moustache, slicked-back hair, oily smirk, white shirt sleeves rolled up, gloved hands. A suave conman riff on the classic Malaysian *Orang Minyak* boogeyman — except this one wears a hat instead of disappearing into the dark.
- **Voice**: smooth, syrupy, calls everyone *kawan / sayang / boss kecik*. Speaks in half-promises. *"Aiyaa kawan, kenapa nak jual kat FatHopes? Saya bagi cash on the spot, no receipt no tax no problem."* Never raises his voice. Smiles when caught.
- **Role in the series**: the *recurring named antagonist*. He's the one Sparron is tracking, the one Kit keeps almost catching, the one D.R. Up has history with. Not in every post — when he shows up, it's a **moment**. Think Team Rocket's Giovanni, not Jessie & James.
- **Running gags**: his white hat keeps getting splashed with oil he himself spilled. He's always on the phone to an off-screen "Mama" who keeps asking when he's coming home for dinner. His jerry-can dent collection is a recurring visual easter egg.
- **How he loses**: never by force. Always by his own greed, or by Sparron's receipts. He escapes at the end of most arcs — *because a series needs its villain alive*. Final defeats are saved for season finales.

### G.O.M. foot soldiers
- Faceless / hooded / interchangeable. Tok Minyak's crew. Oily black gloves, dripping jerry cans, shady lorry at 3am, slick on a longkang. Cartoonish menace. One always slips on his own spilled oil. Beaten by wit, never by fists.

## Interaction model — who's talking to whom

The series is **character-to-character first**. The audience is the *fourth chair at the mamak table*, watching the heroes (and villain) banter. This is what makes a serialised cartoon land — relationships, not monologues.

**Default interaction stack (ranked by frequency across a week):**
1. **Hero ↔ Hero** — most of the series. Kit & Sparron tease each other on comms, D.R. Up calmly resolves their bickering, etc. Chemistry > content.
2. **Hero ↔ Side character** — Kit & a real vendor, Sparron & an auntie-on-the-app, D.R. Up & a PUSH recruit. This is where the *audience sees themselves*.
3. **Hero ↔ Tok Minyak / G.O.M.** — confrontation beats. Witty, never violent.
4. **Side character ↔ Side character** — world-building. Two aunties gossiping about the FatHopes truck rolling up. Adds depth.
5. **Hero → camera (4th-wall break)** — **rationed, see next section.**

### The 4th-wall rule — exactly 2x per week, no more

Breaking the 4th wall is a *flavour*, not a format. Used too often it turns the series into an influencer monologue. Used right, it makes the audience feel *let in on the joke*.

**Quota: 2 breaks per 7-day week. That's it.** Place them where they matter most.

**Who can break the wall, and how:**
- **Kit** — the natural fit. Warm aside to camera mid-scene. *(Kit, mid-pickup, turns to camera: "Korang tengok ni — boss ni nak bagi 5 litre je. Tunggu lah saya pujuk.")* Works because it feels like a friend WhatsApping you a voice note. **Use 1 of the 2 weekly breaks on Kit.**
- **Tok Minyak** — the **best** 4th-wall character. Villains who address the audience are iconic (Frank Underwood, Deadpool, Bugs Bunny villains). A slow turn to camera with a smirk: *"Aiyaa, kawan-kawan… you wouldn't tell on me, right?"* Devastating engagement. **Use the other 1 weekly break on him — usually Wed (when G.O.M. appears).**
- **Sparron** — only in *meta-tech* moments. She might glance at the camera *through* her AR visor — as if she sees the viewer in her dashboard. Rare. **Reserve for arc finales (Fri climax) or season-end.**
- **D.R. Up** — almost never. He's the wise mentor. The day D.R. Up addresses the camera directly is the day something *huge* is happening. **Reserve for big brand moments (SAF launch, milestones).**

**Where 4th-wall breaks land best in the week:**
- **Wed (Villain Sighted)** → Tok Minyak's smirk-to-camera. Caps the cliffhanger.
- **Tue or Sat** → Kit's warm aside. Mid-scene, one sentence, then back to the action.

**What 4th-wall breaks are NOT for:**
- CTAs. Never "follow us" to camera. That's a YouTuber bit, not a series.
- Exposition dumps. If you need to explain the plot, write a better scene.
- Every post. Two a week. Hold the line.

## What makes a post worth engaging with (use this filter on every beat)

A post earns a save/share/comment when it has at least **two** of these:

1. **A character moment people quote** (a catchphrase, a face, a reaction)
2. **A recognisable real-life situation** ("eh, my mamak macam tu jugak")
3. **An open loop** (a question, a cliffhanger, a "to be continued")
4. **A running joke / callback** to a previous episode
5. **A visual moment that's a screenshot** (a face, a slick, a number on Sparron's visor)
6. **A comment bait** that isn't begging — a *thing the audience will instinctively reply to* (e.g., "name your mamak's UCO guy", "guess what G.O.M. is hiding in the jerry can")

If a post hits zero or one — kill it or rebuild it.

## The 7-day arc structure (organic / serial)

A FatHopes week is a **mini-arc**, not 7 disconnected posts. The audience should feel *the week building*. Each week has:
- **A weekly question / hook** (e.g., "What's G.O.M. doing at the new Ampang drain?")
- **A character beat** that pays off mid-week (someone gets a small win or loss)
- **A climax day** where heroes converge (Fri usually)
- **A breathing day** that's just vibes (Sun) — no plot push, just *the world*

## The continuity layer (this is what makes it a series, not 7 posts)

Each week the user plans, write down:
- **One prop** that recurs (a specific jerry can with a dent, Sparron's visor blink pattern, Kit's lucky teh-ais cup)
- **One running gag** for G.O.M. (the cousin texting, the slip-on-own-oil, the boss who's always offscreen)
- **One catchphrase** introduced or repeated per hero (Kit: *"satu titik pun kira"*. D.R. Up: *"dari longkang ke langit"*. Sparron: *"every drop tracked"*)
- **One callback** to a previous week (a returning vendor, a G.O.M. they thought they'd beaten)
- **One tease** at the end of Sunday for next week

Without these the audience watches once and moves on. With them, the audience comes back *looking* for the easter eggs.

## Language — Manglish on purpose

Posts feel like *local content*, not translated corporate. Mix BM + EN inside the same sentence the way Malaysians actually talk:

- **Kit**: heaviest Manglish + BM. *"Boss, satu titik pun kira tau!"*
- **Sparron**: cleaner English, light BM tags. *"Every drop you recycle — kami track semua."*
- **D.R. Up**: BM-leaning when inspirational, English when explaining. *"Dari longkang ke langit — itu bukan magic. Itu chemistry."*

## Captions — organic, not ad-copy

Default caption shape:
```
[1-line in character voice — sets the moment]
[1-line that invites a reply — a question, an "iykyk", a "tag someone"]
#FatHopesEnergy #Fataysia #[hero-tag]
```

What's **out**: phone numbers, URLs, "BOOK NOW", "Sell to us today", price mentions, four hashtags of feature names. None of these per post.
What's **in once per week (Sun)**: a single soft line — *"Wanna join the Fataysia squad? link in bio."* That's it.

Hero hashtags: `#DRUp` `#KitTheSmular` `#Sparron`. Villain: `#TokMinyak` `#GengOrangMinyak`. World: `#Fataysia`.

## Platform priorities

1. **TikTok + Instagram Reels** — where the series lives. 9:16, captioned, music-led.
2. **Facebook** — same Reels reposted; auntie audience is here.
3. **YouTube Shorts** — long-tail discovery; archive of episodes builds the series effect.
4. **WhatsApp Status** — Sunday vibe day repost only.
5. **LinkedIn** — only the Sat hero-spotlight repurposed; B2B audience meets the characters there.

Don't be on all of them every day. Mon–Fri = Reels-first. Sat = IG carousel + LinkedIn. Sun = WhatsApp Status + UGC.

## Production routing

- **Stylised hero shots (Mon, Thu, Sat covers)** → `/nano-banana` keyframes → `/seedance-prompt` motion.
- **Real-vendor scenes (Tue, Wed, Sun)** → real photos for vendors (see [[feedback_real_photos_for_characters]]); heroes composited in the established stylised look. Live shoot where possible — real mamak > AI mamak every time for engagement.
- **Sparron dashboard / decode shots (Thu)** → real screen-record + AR overlay animation.
- **Climax episode (Fri)** → biggest budget. Single long Seedance prompt under 2000 chars (see [[reference_yaroflasher]] and [[feedback_seedance_charlimit]]).

## Weekly theme starters

When the user says "plan a FatHopes week" without a theme, propose 3 of these:

1. **The Longkang Mystery** — a viral oil slick in a real-named area; heroes investigate, G.O.M. revealed.
2. **Ramadan Bazaar Arc** — the night-market is alive; G.O.M. circles; Kit befriends a hawker who becomes a recurring side character.
3. **School Holiday Fry-Up** — kids home, families frying daily; a small auntie character carries the week.
4. **Kit's Origin Week** — flashback to Kit's first ever pickup; recruits a friend who becomes the next PUSH-tease (subtle).
5. **Sparron's Data Day** — Sparron leads the whole week; G.O.M. tries to hack her dashboard and fails comically.
6. **Mamak Map** — Kit visits 7 different mamaks across the week; viewers vote which one becomes a recurring location.
7. **The Plane That Ran on Drops** — D.R. Up week; a SAF-fuelled plane takes off; emotional payoff arc.

## How to apply this

When the user asks for a FatHopes week:

1. Ask **which weekly theme** — or propose 3 from the list above and let them pick.
2. Ask **which hero leads the week's emotional arc** (default: rotate; Kit weeks tend to be highest-engagement).
3. Ask **language mix** (default: Manglish, Kit heavier BM, Sparron heavier EN).
4. Build the 7-day plan using the template — fill every row with: **hero, hook (one line in voice), 3-5 shot beats, caption draft (entertainment-first), engagement mechanic, production note** (which model / live shoot).
5. Mark the **climax day** explicitly.
6. Write the **continuity layer** at the top of the plan: one prop, one running gag, one catchphrase, one callback, one next-week tease.
7. **Allocate the 2 weekly 4th-wall breaks** — mark which posts get them and which character delivers (default: Tok Minyak on Wed, Kit on Tue or Sat).
8. **Tag the interaction type** on every post (Hero↔Hero / Hero↔Side / Hero↔Tok / Side↔Side / 4th-wall). A healthy week is mostly Hero↔Hero and Hero↔Side, with Tok appearing 1–2x.
9. Keep **exactly one soft-CTA**, on Sunday only. Strip CTAs from every other day.
10. End with **2 next-week tease options** so the series chains.

When critiquing an existing FatHopes week / draft:

1. Run every post through the **engagement filter** (2 of the 6 mechanics minimum). Flag posts that hit 0–1.
2. Check **voice fidelity** per hero against the lane table. Flag any line a different hero would say.
3. Check the **arc** — is there a mystery → build → climax → breather? If posts are isolated, suggest the continuity layer.
4. Check **Tok Minyak presence** — 1–2 named appearances per week; cartoonish, never preachy. If only faceless G.O.M. show up and Tok is absent, the series loses its main rival.
5. **4th-wall audit** — count breaks. More than 2 = cut to 2. Zero = add 2 (default placements: Wed/Tok, Tue or Sat/Kit).
6. **Interaction audit** — if more than 2 posts are hero-monologues (no other character to bounce off), rewrite. The series runs on relationships.
7. **CTA audit** — if more than one post has a hard CTA, cut them. The whole point is organic.
8. Check the **continuity layer** — prop, gag, catchphrase, callback, tease. Add what's missing.
9. Give 3 ranked edits — structural before cosmetic.
