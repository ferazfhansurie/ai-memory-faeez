For branded / business short-form content (PUSH Life, ADletic client work, FatHopes, MotionBoards — NOT the @faeezree personal brand, which stays raw/unscripted per [[feedback_tiktok_unscripted]]), Firaz wants a default **Gen-Z "funny but kinda serious"** style.

**Why:** the formats that win for brand Reels in 2025-26 aren't pure gags — they're The Office / Corporate Natalie style: funny *because it's recognisable and a little true*. Gen-Z distrusts polished ads (~8x more trust in real-people/EGC content); the brand should be the setting and the butt of the joke, never an interruptive plug. Pure comedy with a grounded/heartfelt turn lands warmer and converts better.

**How to apply (the formula):**
- **Comedy → a real turn.** Start funny/absurd, then pivot to a small genuine point or heart beat (e.g. the "useless Gen-Z hire" is actually competent — gap is style, not ability). Never preachy.
- **Recurring characters + 2 engineered catchphrases** (one per character) designed for comment-section quoting. Run it as a numbered episodic SHOW, not one-offs.
- **Funny-but-serious, not slapstick** — deadpan, ironic, recognition humor. Manglish + code-switching (boss heavy BM, Gen-Z drops English brainrot slang = the language gap is its own joke).
- **Production:** lo-fi, phone-shot, real people; seamless loop end→hook; hook in <3s with on-frame text; captions always on (centre 70%); ONE recipient-based CTA ("tag your boss") + "follow for Ep 2"; optimise completion/sends, not likes.
- **Audio licensing (business accounts):** can't legally use trending commercial songs — use ORIGINAL audio (e.g. character VO; you own it if it trends) or Meta Sound Collection; recreate viral dance *moves* over a cleared track.
- Use [[feedback_real_photos_for_characters]] when generating: real people for characters, AI for objects/environments only.
