# The Reel Tempo Formula

A reel is engineered, not improvised. Every second has a job. Below is the structural breakdown and the rules of thumb that make a reel hold attention to the loop point.

## Optimal length

- **30-45 seconds** is the sweet spot for organic reach in 2026. Watch-time-to-length ratio peaks here.
- **60-90s** only if it's a tutorial people will rewatch — completion rate drops fast past 45s for cold traffic.
- **15-25s** for top-of-funnel hook content meant to drive profile visits.

## The 7 beats (timestamps assume a 40-second reel — scale proportionally)

| Beat | Timing | What goes here | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Hook | 0.0 - 1.5s | Pattern interrupt — bold claim, weird visual, question, contradiction | The algorithm decides if you scroll past in this window. Most reels die here. |
| 2. Promise | 1.5 - 3s | What they'll get if they stay. "Here's how I…" / "Watch this transform into…" | Buys the next 5 seconds of attention. |
| 3. Setup | 3 - 8s | Context, the problem, the stakes. One sentence max. | Frames why the payoff matters. Skip if the hook already implied it. |
| 4. Build | 8 - 24s | The meat. Steps, demo, breakdown, story beats. | Hardest part to keep moving. Cut every 1-2s, layer text on every shot. |
| 5. **Climax** | **24 - 32s (around 60-75% mark)** | **The payoff. The reveal. The "wow."** Music drop, color shift, faster cuts, biggest reaction. | This is what they screenshot and share. Without it the reel is forgettable. |
| 6. Resolution | 32 - 36s | Quick recap or "and that's how…" — give the audience a moment to land. | Stops the climax from feeling rushed. Pace down briefly. |
| 7. CTA + Loop | 36 - 40s | "Follow for more" / "link in bio" + a final shot that visually rhymes with the opening | The loop is the secret. If frame 40s leads cleanly into frame 0s, viewers rewatch involuntarily and watch-time goes up. |

## Where the climax goes (the most important rule)

**Climax sits at the 60-75% mark.** Not the end. Never the end.

- Too early (before 50%): viewers leave once the payoff is delivered.
- At the end (95-100%): no time for resolution, viewer drops at the cliff and doesn't loop.
- **At ~70%:** they get the payoff, you give them 8-12s of resolution and CTA, and the loop pulls them back in.

For a 40s reel, climax frame is around the **27-30 second mark.**
For a 60s reel, climax frame is around the **40-42 second mark.**
For a 20s reel, climax frame is around the **14 second mark.**

## Where the intro goes

The intro is **NOT a logo splash**. The intro is the **first 1.5 seconds of the hook**. Logos at the start kill 50%+ of viewers.

If a brand splash is required, put it at the **resolution** beat (after the climax) when the viewer is committed and rewarded — they'll forgive the branding then.

## Cut tempo (tied to music BPM)

| Section | Shot length | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | 0.5-1s shots | Sets the tempo; trains viewer to expect speed |
| Setup | 1-2s shots | Slightly slower so viewer can absorb context |
| Build | 1-1.5s shots | Steady, snappy, music sync |
| **Climax** | **0.3-0.7s shots** | **Fastest section. Music drop syncs with cuts. Maximum sensory load.** |
| Resolution | 1.5-2s shots | Breath. Viewer recovers. |
| CTA | 1-2s shot, often a single sustained shot with text overlay | Calm so the CTA reads |

A reel feels "professional" when cuts land on music beats. Use a track at 100-130 BPM, cut every beat or every other beat. Trending audio is critical — search the platform's trending sounds before scripting.

## Text on screen rules

- **80%+ of viewers watch muted.** Never rely on audio alone.
- Text appears on every shot. Big, bold, max 6 words.
- Place text in the **middle 60% of the frame** — Reels/TikTok crop top and bottom for the UI.
- Animate it in (slide, scale, type-on) — a static text box looks amateur.
- Use the same font + color throughout for brand consistency.

## Aspect ratio + safe zones

- Always **9:16 vertical** (1080×1920).
- Keep critical content in the **center 1080×1500 box** — top/bottom 200px each get covered by platform UI (caption, like button, sound name).

## The opening frame test

Pause your reel on frame 1. Ask:
1. **Does this make me curious enough to keep watching?** (visual mystery, action mid-motion, unexpected face)
2. **Is text on screen telling me what I'm about to see?**
3. **Does it look weird/different from the surrounding feed?** (a generic landscape shot blends into infinity scroll — a face zoomed in, a hand reaching for something, a glitch transition stops the thumb)

If any answer is no, re-shoot frame 1.

## How to apply this

When the user asks for a reel script:

1. Ask the **goal** (sell? educate? entertain? drive profile visits?).
2. Ask the **length target** (default 40s if not specified).
3. Generate a beat-by-beat sheet using the table above, with concrete shot descriptions and exact text overlays.
4. Mark the climax timestamp explicitly.
5. Suggest a music style + BPM range.
6. Suggest the loop frame (how the last shot connects back to the first).

When critiquing an existing reel transcript:

1. Identify which beat each section maps to.
2. Find the climax. Is it at 60-75%? If not, suggest where to move it.
3. Spot dead air (any 3+ seconds without a cut, music change, text update, or motion).
4. Spot the loop — does the end visually connect to the start?
5. Give 3 specific edits ranked by impact.

## Reference: the MotionBoards intro applied to this formula

The Faeez MotionBoards intro (76s) maps roughly to:
- Hook (0-3s): "Yo, FAEEZ here and motionboards" — works
- Setup (3-13s): "one canvas, drop everything…" — slightly long
- **Climax (13-24s): the rap interlude** — strong, but at ~25% mark which is too early; the actual payoff (the generated video at 41-54s) is the real climax
- Build (24-41s): explanation of image-to-video — slow
- True climax (41-54s): video playback at ~70% mark — correct position
- CTA (65-76s): "click the link below" — too long

**Verdict:** the rap is great but is being treated as the hook when it's really the climax. A re-cut would: keep "Yo FAEEZ here" + a 2-second teaser of the final video as the hook, run the explanation as the build, drop the rap+final-video reveal at the 60-70% climax mark, end with a 5-second CTA + loop.
